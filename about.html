About this site

What Is StateOfRochester?

StateOfRochester is an independent, community-driven news site dedicated to covering the issues, events, and developments that shape daily life in Rochester, New York. Founded in 2025, this site exists because local journalism matters — and because Rochester deserves a clear, honest account of what is happening within its neighborhoods, institutions, and government.

"The goal is simple: give Rochester residents the clearest possible picture of what's going on in their city, without spin and without agenda."

At the center of this site is the Community Crisis Meter — a real-time indicator of the city's overall state, updated regularly based on local reports, public data, and community input. It is not a scientific index, but a practical, at-a-glance tool for residents who want to know, at any given moment, whether Rochester is in a period of calm or concern.

Beyond the meter, StateOfRochester publishes original reporting on city politics, public safety, infrastructure, schools, and community life. All coverage is local. All coverage is free.

Editorial Principles

This site operates according to a small set of principles that guide every editorial decision:

01

Independence

StateOfRochester is not affiliated with any political party, government body, or corporate interest. Coverage is not for sale.

02

Accuracy First

Every effort is made to verify information before publication. Corrections are issued promptly and transparently when errors occur.

03

Community-Driven

Readers are the most important source of local knowledge. Tips, leads, and meter feedback from the community directly shape this site's coverage.

About the Crisis Meter

The Crisis Meter is updated manually whenever there is a meaningful shift in the overall state of community affairs. It takes into account factors such as public safety incidents, infrastructure disruptions, political or institutional instability, public health concerns, and the general mood reported by community members.

The meter is intentionally subjective — it is a synthesis, not an algorithm. Community members are encouraged to submit their own observations and recommendations through the Tips page. All feedback is reviewed and considered when the meter is updated.

How to Get in Touch

Whether you have a tip about a developing story, a correction to report, or a perspective on where the Crisis Meter should be set, this site wants to hear from you. Use the Tips & Contact page to reach out. All messages are read.
